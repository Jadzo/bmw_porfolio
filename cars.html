Newest BMW Cars

BMW 5 Series

Executive car (E)

The BMW 5 Series, a prominent executive car, has been a flagship offering from BMW since 1972, succeeding the New Class Sedans. Now in its seventh generation, it has evolved over the years, initially starting as a sedan and later expanding to include a wagon/estate ("Touring") in 1991 and a 5-door fastback ("Gran Turismo") from 2009 to 2017. Each generation has its internal E-designation, and the lineup has seen a variety of engine options, including naturally aspirated four-cylinders, six-cylinders, V8s, and even V10s, both naturally aspirated and turbocharged, alongside diesel engines since 1982. This model holds significant importance for BMW, being the second-best-selling in their lineup after the 3 Series, and it marked the beginning of BMW's three-digit model naming convention, making it the pioneer in utilizing "Series" in its name. Notably, on January 29, 2008, BMW celebrated the production of the 5 millionth 5 Series vehicle, a 530d sedan in Carbon Black Metallic.

BMW X5

Midsize Sport Utility Vehicle

The BMW X5 is a mid-size luxury SUV that marked BMW's entry into the SUV market when it debuted in 1999 as the E53 model. This launch featured all-wheel drive and manual or automatic transmission options. The second generation, known as the E70, was introduced in 2006 and introduced the xDrive all-wheel drive system with an automatic gearbox. In 2009, the high-performance X5 M variant was released. BMW officially termed the X5 as a "Sports Activity Vehicle" (SAV) rather than an SUV to highlight its on-road handling capabilities despite its larger size. It was a pioneer in shifting from body-on-frame to monocoque chassis construction. The X5 is primarily manufactured in North America, with assembly in various other countries, including Russia, India, Indonesia, Malaysia, and Thailand. This model also served as the foundation for BMW's expansion into the SAV series, including the X3 and X6.

BMW IX

All-Electric Sports Utility Vehicle

The BMW iX is a noteworthy mid-size luxury crossover SUV in BMW's lineup, representing a significant milestone in the German automaker's electric vehicle journey. Initially revealed as the Vision iNext concept at the 2018 Paris Motor Show, it transitioned into a fully production-ready vehicle by November 2020. Notably, the iX is BMW's first purpose-built electric vehicle since the i3 introduced in 2013 and serves as the fourth model in the BMW i sub-brand. The choice of the "iX" nameplate reflects its status as the flagship electric vehicle in BMW's lineup, showcasing the latest fifth-generation electric drive system technology while offering advanced autonomous and connected features.

BMW 4 Series Convertable

Compact executive car (D)

The BMW 4 Series, introduced in 2013 and currently in its second generation, represents a range of compact executive cars that stemmed from BMW's decision to separate the 2-door models (coupé and convertible) of the 3 Series into a distinct series. Initially unveiled as a concept at the 2013 North American International Auto Show, the 4 Series encompasses coupé, convertible, and 5-door liftback (marketed as "Gran Coupé") body styles. Engine options for the first generation included turbocharged inline-3, inline-4, and inline-6 engines, running on either petrol or diesel, with the second generation introducing a plug-in hybrid powertrain. The drivetrain layout mirrors that of the equivalent 3 Series generations, offering rear-wheel drive or all-wheel drive ("xDrive"). For enthusiasts seeking high performance, the BMW M4 serves as the high-performance variant, featuring a turbocharged inline-six petrol engine similar to the M3.

BMW M2 Coupe

Subcompact executive car/Sport compact (C)

The BMW 2 Series, introduced in 2014, is a versatile range of subcompact executive cars that emerged as a distinct series when BMW separated the 2-door models (coupé and convertible) from the BMW 1 Series. Initially launched as 2-door coupés and convertibles on a rear-wheel-drive platform, the 2 Series family expanded to include an Active Tourer 5-seat compact MPV and a 7-seat Gran Tourer, both built on a front-wheel-drive platform shared with the Mini Hatch. In 2019, the Gran Coupé fastback sedan joined as a front-wheel-drive vehicle based on the 1 Series hatchback. In 2021, BMW released the second-generation 2-door coupé, featuring rear-wheel drive. For performance enthusiasts, the BMW M2 serves as the high-performance variant, boasting turbocharged straight-six engines in its first-generation F87 coupé model.

BMW 7 Series

Plug-in Hybrid

The BMW 7 Series, in production since 1977 and now in its seventh generation, is BMW's flagship luxury sedan known for pioneering technologies and engine options, including high-performance variants like the BMW M760 6.6L V12 and the Alpina B7 (G12).

BMW Z4

Electric Vehicle

The first-gen BMW Z4 (E85/E86), produced from 2002 to 2008, succeeded the Z3. It included a roadster and coupe, both made in South Carolina. The high-performance Z4 M had an S54 straight-six engine. In 2009, it was succeeded by the BMW Z4 (E89).

BMW i8

Premium Coupe

The BMW i8, a plug-in hybrid sports car released in 2014, featured a 0-100 km/h time of 4.4 seconds and a top speed of 250 km/h. Initially, it had a 37 km electric range, later extended to 55 km in 2018. It achieved impressive fuel efficiency at 2.1 L/100 km and production concluded in June 2020.

BMW X6 M

Premium Coupe

The BMW X6, a mid-size luxury crossover SUV, introduced the sports activity coupé (SAC) concept with its distinct sloping roof design. It combines SUV functionality with coupé aesthetics and shares a platform with the X5. Initially a flagship model, the first generation debuted in 2008, followed by the second in 2014, and the third in 2019.
